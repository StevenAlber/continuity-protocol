Not a panic product. A protocol layer.

Every competitor either alerts you that something bad is happening or removes you after things are already severe. Continuity Protocol occupies the missing layer in between: exact household-specific next steps when travel, finance, safety, or infrastructure begin to break down.

Layer 1

Household profile

Family structure, passports, travel rights, medications, fallback destinations, and the minimum continuity context required to personalize action.

Layer 2 + 3

Location + shock detection

Your current corridor, nearest usable exit nodes, and live signals across flights, conflicts, disasters, telecom issues, and route stress.

Layer 4 + 5

Protocol + continuity

Do this now. Do this in 6 hours. Do this in 24 hours. If A closes, switch to B. Keep docs and family status ready even when the network degrades.

Three modes. One calm rhythm.

Most crisis products live at the extremes: either everything is fine or everything is panic. Continuity Protocol is built around the middle zone where decisions actually happen.

Green — Peacetime

Readiness dashboard, document expiry tracking, route resilience, quiet signals, and family drills. The app earns its keep on a normal Tuesday.

Amber — Watch

Something relevant changed. You get the exact implications for your household and what to prepare in the next 24 hours. No theatrics. Just preparation.

Red — Protocol Active

Threshold crossed. Now / 6 hours / 24 hours. Fallback branches. Family notification. Offline access. Confidence and freshness on every card.

What we never promise

This is an information and decision-support service. It is not evacuation, not insurance, not medical advice, and not a guarantee that any route or border is passable in the last mile.

  • Advisory only. Conditions may differ from available data.
  • Every protocol is labeled with confidence and freshness.
  • No stored banking passwords, payment credentials, or fantasy claims.
