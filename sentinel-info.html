What Sentinel gives you

Sentinel is not a news feed. It is a private intelligence layer that monitors your regions, filters for signals that affect mobile families, and tells you when your continuity posture shifts.

Every signal card answers one question: does this change what my family should do?

  • Live regional signal monitoring — Gulf & Middle East, Southeast Asia, Europe, East Asia. Signal refresh every 15–30 minutes.
  • Protocol-impact alerts — not "something happened" but "this changes your risk posture." Flight disruption, border friction, fuel pressure, route stress, conflict escalation.
  • Risk posture tracking — your status moves through four states: Watch → Prepare → Act → Switch. Each shift comes with specific recommended actions.
  • Daily continuity digest — top changes in your regions, why they matter, what to watch next. Confidence labels on every signal.
  • Route fragility awareness — corridor stress, airline reliability, fuel impact, transit hub pressure. See whether your exit paths are strong, softening, or brittle.
  • Monthly protocol refresh — your personalized crisis protocol stays current. Updated with latest route, border, and risk data each month.
  • Priority response — direct email support, faster turnaround on questions and protocol updates.

What Sentinel monitors

Signals are filtered for relevance to internationally mobile families. Not everything that happens — only what changes your posture.

  • Flight disruption — cancellations, rerouting, airspace closures, carrier reliability
  • Border & admin friction — visa changes, processing delays, entry restrictions
  • Fuel pressure — pricing spikes, supply stress, impact on route viability
  • Conflict & escalation — military developments, threat rhetoric, security posture changes
  • Route stress — corridor congestion, transit hub pressure, alternative path availability
  • Embassy & advisory lag — when official guidance trails reality on the ground
  • Telecom & infrastructure — communication disruptions, payment system availability

Every signal carries a confidence label: Confirmed, Likely, or Emerging. We tell you what we know and what we don't.
